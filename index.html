Why Commercial Vehicle Accidents Are Different

Commercial vehicle accident claims typically result in higher settlements compared to regular passenger vehicle accidents. Here's why:

Factor Regular Car Accidents Commercial Vehicle Accidents
Insurance Limits Generally lower personal policy limits (typically $15K-$50K) Higher commercial policy limits (often $1M+)
Liable Parties Typically just the individual driver Multiple potential liable parties (driver, employer, maintenance company)
Regulations Standard traffic laws Additional federal and state regulations for commercial vehicles
Evidence Available Limited (police report, witnesses) More extensive (driver logs, maintenance records, black box data)
Typical Settlement Range $10,000 - $100,000 $50,000 - $500,000+

Frequently Asked Questions

Am I eligible if I was hit by a commercial vehicle?
Yes, if you were injured in an accident where a commercial or work vehicle hit you, you may be eligible for significant compensation. This includes accidents involving delivery trucks, service vans, company cars, construction vehicles, 18-wheelers, and even rideshare vehicles being used for work purposes.
How much compensation could I qualify for?
Compensation varies based on factors like injury severity, medical expenses, lost wages, and liability. Commercial vehicle accidents often qualify for higher settlements due to commercial insurance policies and potential employer liability. Many of our clients have received settlements ranging from $350,000 to over $1,500,000.
Do I need to pay anything upfront?
No. Our partner attorneys work on a No Win, No Fee basis, meaning you pay nothing unless they win your case. There are no upfront costs, consultation fees, or hidden charges. You only pay a percentage of your settlement after you receive compensation.
What if the accident was partially my fault?
Depending on your state's laws, you may still be eligible for compensation even if you were partially at fault. This is known as comparative negligence. For example, if you were 20% at fault, you might still recover 80% of your damages. Our attorneys can evaluate your specific situation.
How long does a commercial vehicle accident claim take?
Most commercial vehicle accident claims are resolved within 6-18 months, depending on the complexity of the case and the parties involved. Simple cases may settle faster, while complex cases involving multiple parties or severe injuries might take longer.
Why are commercial vehicle accidents different from regular accidents?
Commercial vehicle accidents typically involve higher insurance policy limits, potential employer liability, and are subject to additional federal and state regulations. This often means more potential compensation for victims. Additionally, companies have significant resources to investigate accidents quickly, making it important for you to seek legal help promptly.
What if I was a pedestrian or cyclist hit by a work vehicle?
Pedestrians and cyclists hit by work vehicles often have strong claims for compensation. These cases may involve significant injuries and clear liability against the commercial driver and their employer. Our network of attorneys has extensive experience representing pedestrians and cyclists in these situations.
What types of damages can I recover?
You may be eligible to recover medical expenses (past and future), lost wages, loss of earning capacity, pain and suffering, emotional distress, property damage, and in some cases, punitive damages. Our attorneys work to maximize all available compensation sources.
